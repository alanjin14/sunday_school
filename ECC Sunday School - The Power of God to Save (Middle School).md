# Sunday School: The Power of God to Save
### ECC Redmond | March 1, 2026 Sermon Review
### For Middle Schoolers (Grades 6-8) | 1 Hour Session

---

## LEADER'S OVERVIEW

**Main Scripture:** Romans 1:16-17
**Theme:** The gospel is the power of God to save — and it works through faith, not our own effort.
**Goal:** Students understand what salvation really means (past, present, future), and that faith — not being "good enough" — connects us to God's power.

## ICEBREAKER GAME: "POWER RANKINGS" (8 min)

### How to Play
Give each student a sheet of paper (or do this as a group on a whiteboard). Read the following list of "powers" out loud. Students must **rank them from 1 (most powerful) to 8 (least powerful)** based on what they think matters most in life.

**The List:**
- 💰 Money — you can buy anything
- 🧠 Intelligence — you're the smartest person alive
- 💪 Super strength — you can lift a car
- 📱 Influence — 100 million followers on social media
- ⏰ Time travel — go anywhere in time
- 🛡️ Invincibility — nothing can hurt you
- 👑 Political power — you're president of the world
- ❤️ The power to change someone's heart

**After ranking:**
- Ask a few students to share their #1 pick and why.
- Ask: *"Which of these powers can last forever? Which ones disappear when you die?"*
- Transition: *"Today's sermon was about a power that's bigger than ALL of these — and it actually lasts forever. Let's talk about it."*

### Main Point #1: The Gospel Is Nothing to Be Embarrassed About

**Talk through it like this:**

Imagine you're at school, and someone asks, "Hey, what did you do this weekend?" And you went to church. Do you feel a little awkward saying that? Maybe some of you do, maybe you don't — but that feeling is what Paul was dealing with, except WAY bigger.

Paul was heading to *Rome* — the most powerful city in the world. Think of it like Washington D.C., New York, and Hollywood all combined. Everyone there believed in power, strength, and winning.

And Paul shows up saying: *"I follow a guy who washed people's feet, hung out with the outcasts, and died on a cross."*

In Roman culture, the cross was the most humiliating, disgusting way to die. It was reserved for the worst criminals. Imagine telling the cool kids at school that your hero was someone who got publicly humiliated — and that's actually a GOOD thing.

But Paul says: **"I'm not ashamed."** Why? Because he had personally experienced how powerful the gospel is. Paul used to HATE Christians — he literally had them thrown in prison and killed. Then Jesus showed up, and Paul's entire life flipped 180 degrees. He went from being the #1 enemy of Christians to their biggest champion.

That kind of change? That's not will power. That's not a self-help book. That's **God power.**

**Key idea:** *The gospel looks weak to the world, but it's actually the most powerful force that exists.*

### Main Point #2: God's Power Can Save You — Past, Present, and Future

**Talk through it like this:**

When we hear "saved," we usually think of one moment — like accepting Jesus. But the Bible actually talks about salvation in THREE stages. Think of it like a superhero movie trilogy:

### 📺 Video: "3 Ways GOD Saves Us from Sin" (5 min) ⭐ RECOMMENDED
Animated, super simple explanation of justification, sanctification, and glorification — perfect intro before walking through the three stages.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N1r7Ts7zUFg
*Play this first, then walk through each "movie" below with your own commentary.*

**🎬 Movie 1: JUSTIFICATION — "You Are Rescued" (Past — happened once)**

This is the moment God says: "You're mine now. You're forgiven. You're not guilty anymore."

Think of it this way: Imagine you owe a billion dollars in debt (that's your sin). You can NEVER pay it back. Then someone walks in and says, "I'll pay it all." That's what Jesus did on the cross. Your record? Wiped clean. Your status? Changed from "guilty" to "forgiven." From "outsider" to "family."

You didn't earn it. You can't earn it. It's a gift.

**🎬 Movie 2: SANCTIFICATION — "You Are Being Upgraded" (Present — happening now)**

After you're saved, God doesn't just leave you. The Holy Spirit moves IN and starts working on you from the inside out — like a software update that never stops.

The Holy Spirit:
- Gives you a nudge when you're about to do something wrong (like a conscience on steroids)
- Helps you resist temptation — God promises He will NEVER let you be tempted beyond what you can handle (1 Cor 10:13)
- Protects you from dangers you don't even know about
- Puts people in your life to help you grow

This doesn't mean life is easy or you stop messing up. It means God is actively working in you every single day.

**🎬 Movie 3: GLORIFICATION — "The Final Level" (Future — coming someday)**

One day, when Jesus comes back or when we go to be with Him, everything gets fully restored. New body, new world, no more sin, no more pain. Game over — the good guys win permanently.

**Key idea:** *Salvation isn't just a one-time ticket to heaven. God is saving you right now, today, and He'll finish the job.*

### Main Point #3: This Power Runs on Faith, Not Effort

**Talk through it like this:**

Here's the part that messes with everyone's brain — including adults.

We're used to earning things: good grades come from studying, being good at sports comes from practice, getting paid comes from working. So naturally we think: "I need to be good enough for God."

But the gospel flips that upside down.

Paul says the righteous will **live by faith** — not by being perfect, not by checking boxes, not by earning it.

Remember the thief on the cross next to Jesus? This guy was a criminal. He had NOTHING to offer. He couldn't even move — he was literally dying. All he said was: *"Jesus, remember me when you come into your kingdom."*

And Jesus said: *"Today you will be with me in paradise."*

That's it. No church attendance record. No volunteer hours. No perfect behavior streak. Just faith.

Does that mean we can do whatever we want? No — because when you truly trust God, the Holy Spirit changes what you WANT. But the entry point is always faith, and the fuel that keeps it going is faith too. "From faith to faith," as Paul says.

**Think of it like charging your phone.** Your phone is amazing — camera, apps, games, everything. But if it's not plugged in, it's just a brick. **Faith is what plugs you into God's power.** Without it, you're running on your own battery, and that runs out fast.

**Key idea:** *Faith — not being good enough — is what connects you to God's power.*

## GAME: "BEFORE & AFTER" (10 min)

### How to Play

This game shows how the gospel changes everything — specifically how Paul's life changed, and how salvation changes our identity.

**Round 1: Bible Edition**
Split into 2 teams. Read a "BEFORE" statement. Teams race to shout out (or write down) the "AFTER." First team to get it right scores a point.

| BEFORE (without God) | AFTER (with God) |
|---|---|
| Paul persecuted Christians | Paul preached the gospel and defended Christians |
| Enemies of God | Friends of God |
| Orphans | Adopted into God's family |
| Condemned / Guilty | Forgiven |
| Unrighteous | Declared righteous |
| Hearts of stone | Hearts of flesh (soft, responsive to God) |
| Dead in sin | Made alive in Christ |
| Slaves to sin | Free in Christ |
| Fearful | Courageous through the Holy Spirit |
| Headed for separation from God | Headed for eternal life with God |

**Round 2: Real Life Edition**
Read a scenario. Teams discuss for 30 seconds, then share how the gospel might change the "before" to an "after."

Scenarios:
1. *"A kid at school constantly bullies others because they feel powerless at home."*
2. *"Someone is obsessed with getting likes on social media because they feel worthless."*
3. *"A student cheats on every test because they're terrified of failure."*
4. *"Someone acts like they don't need anyone because they've been let down too many times."*

**Point:** The gospel doesn't just change our afterlife destination — it changes who we are RIGHT NOW.

## GAME: "SALVATION TIMELINE RACE" (7 min)

### Setup
Prepare 3 sets of cards (one set per team). Each set has **9 cards** — 3 cards for each stage of salvation. Shuffle each set.

### The Cards

**JUSTIFICATION cards (write "J" on back):**
- "Your sins are forgiven — once and for all"
- "You are declared righteous by God"
- "You are adopted into God's family"

**SANCTIFICATION cards (write "S" on back):**
- "The Holy Spirit helps you resist temptation daily"
- "God brings people into your life to help you grow"
- "You are being transformed more and more like Jesus"

**GLORIFICATION cards (write "G" on back):**
- "You receive a new, perfect body"
- "Jesus returns and makes all things new"
- "You are with God forever — no more tears or pain"

### How to Play
1. Split into 2-3 teams
2. Give each team a shuffled set of 9 cards
3. On "GO," teams must sort the cards into three groups under the correct header: **Justification / Sanctification / Glorification**
4. First team to sort all 9 correctly wins!

**Debrief question:** *"Which of these three stages are you living in right now?"* (Answer: Sanctification — we've been justified, and we're waiting for glorification, but right now the Holy Spirit is working in us every day.)

## SMALL GROUP DISCUSSION (15 min)

Split into groups of 4-5. Assign a leader to each group (older student or adult). Pick 4-5 questions from below based on your group.

### Opening Questions (Pick 1)

1. **Have you ever felt embarrassed or awkward about being a Christian?** What happened? How did you handle it? How does Paul's example encourage you?

2. **If someone at school said, "Why do you even go to church? That's so lame," what would you say?** (No pressure to have a perfect answer — let's think about it together.)

### Going Deeper (Pick 2-3)

3. **The sermon said we're all capable of doing terrible things — even people raised in good families.** Do you agree or disagree? Why do you think that is? How does that make the gospel more important?

4. **What's the difference between "trying to be a good person" and "living by faith"?** Why does God care more about faith than our good behavior?

5. **The sermon compared God's power to the power of Rome / America — money, connections, education, influence.** What kinds of "power" do you see your classmates chasing? What "power" do you find yourself wanting most? How is God's power different?

6. **Read 1 Corinthians 10:13 together:**
   > *"No temptation has overtaken you except what is common to mankind. And God is faithful; he will not let you be tempted beyond what you can bear."*

   Do you believe this? Can you think of a time God helped you through a tough situation or temptation, even if you didn't realize it at the time?

7. **The thief on the cross had nothing to offer Jesus — he just believed.** Why is it so hard for us to accept that faith is enough? What do we keep trying to "add" to faith?

8. **The sermon said, "Faith plugs us into the power of God."** If faith is like plugging in your phone, what are the things that "unplug" us in daily life? What can we do to stay connected?

### For Groups Ready to Go Deeper

9. **The sermon mentioned the US bombing Iran and said the gospel is connected to world events, not disconnected.** How does being saved by God connect to caring about injustice, war, and suffering in the world? What does it look like for US — middle schoolers — to be "salt and light"?

10. **The Catholic priest at the funeral asked: "You all know where you are from, but do you know where you're going? Are you sure? And do you like where you are going?"** If someone asked you that question right now, what would you honestly say?

## WRAP-UP & PRAYER (5 min)

### One-Sentence Takeaway
Go around the circle. Each student finishes this sentence:

> **"One thing I learned or want to remember this week is _______________."**

### Challenge for the Week
Pick ONE of these to try this week:

- 📖 **Read Romans 1:16-17 every morning** and ask God, "Help me not be ashamed of you today."
- 🙏 **When you're tempted to do something wrong**, pause and say, "God, I trust you — help me through this." (That's sanctification in action!)
- 💬 **If someone asks what you did this weekend**, be honest: "I went to church." See what happens.
- 📝 **Write down one thing** you're trusting God with right now — something you can't control. Put it somewhere you'll see it every day.

### Closing Prayer
*"God, thank you that your power is bigger than anything we face — bigger than school drama, bigger than our mistakes, bigger than our fears. Thank you that you don't ask us to be perfect — you just ask us to believe. Help us to trust you this week, even when it's hard. Help us not to be ashamed of you. And thank you that you are saving us — not just once, but every single day. In Jesus' name, amen."*

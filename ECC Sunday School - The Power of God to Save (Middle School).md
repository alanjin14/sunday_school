
# Sunday School: The Power of God to Save
### ECC Redmond | March 1, 2026 Sermon Review  
### For Middle Schoolers (Grades 6–8) | 1 Hour Session

---

## LEADER'S OVERVIEW

**Main Scripture:** Romans 1:16-17  
**Theme:** The gospel is the power of God to save — and it works through faith, not our own effort.  
**Goal:** Students understand what salvation really means (past, present, future), and that faith — not being "good enough" — connects us to God's power.

This lesson intentionally **builds on the Sunday sermon**, helping students review key ideas, ask questions, and process what they heard in a smaller group setting.

# ICEBREAKER GAME: "POWER RANKINGS" (8 min)

### How to Play

Give each student a sheet of paper (or do this as a group on a whiteboard). Read the following list of "powers" out loud. Students must **rank them from 1 (most powerful) to 8 (least powerful)** based on what they think matters most in life.

**The List:**

- 💰 Money — you can buy anything  
- 🧠 Intelligence — you're the smartest person alive  
- 💪 Super strength — you can lift a car  
- 📱 Influence — 100 million followers on social media  
- ⏰ Time travel — go anywhere in time  
- 🛡️ Invincibility — nothing can hurt you  
- 👑 Political power — you're president of the world  
- ❤️ The power to change someone's heart  

After ranking:

Ask a few students:

- What did you put as #1?
- Why?

Then ask:

**"Which of these powers actually lasts forever?"**

Transition:

> "In Sunday's sermon we heard that the gospel is the *power of God to save*. Today we're going to talk about why that power is greater than all the others."

# SERMON REVIEW & TEACHING (10–12 min)

The goal here is **not to reteach the entire sermon**, but to help students **remember the key ideas** and prepare them for discussion.

### Read Together

Romans 1:16–17

> "For I am not ashamed of the gospel, because it is the power of God that brings salvation to everyone who believes..."

Ask students:

- What words stand out to you in this verse?

---

## Key Idea 1 — The Gospel Is God's Power

Paul says the gospel is **the power of God to save**.

But it doesn't look powerful.

Jesus died on a cross — something that looked weak and shameful to the Roman world.

Yet through that, God changes lives.

Paul himself went from **persecuting Christians** to **becoming one of the greatest missionaries ever**.

That kind of change is not self-improvement.

That is **God's power**.

---

## Key Idea 2 — Salvation Is Bigger Than One Moment

Salvation has **three parts**:

**Justification (Past)**  
God forgives our sins and declares us righteous.

**Sanctification (Present)**  
God is changing us little by little every day.

**Glorification (Future)**  
One day Jesus will make everything new.

---

## Key Idea 3 — This Power Works Through Faith

We often think we must **earn God's approval**.

But the Bible says:

> "The righteous will live by faith."

Faith means **trusting Jesus**, not trying to be perfect.

Faith connects us to God's power.

# SMALL GROUP DISCUSSION (40 min)

Split students into groups of **4–5 with one leader**.

Leaders should focus on **conversation rather than rushing through questions**.

Encourage students to **share honestly and ask questions**.

---

## Warm-Up Questions

1. What part of Sunday's sermon do you remember the most?

2. Was there anything from the sermon that confused you or made you curious?

3. Why do you think Paul said he was **"not ashamed of the gospel"?**

---

## Understanding the Gospel

4. What do people your age usually think gives someone "power"?

5. How is **God's power** different from those things?

6. Why do you think it can be embarrassing sometimes to say you're a Christian?

---

## Faith vs Effort

7. What is the difference between **trying to be a good person** and **trusting God by faith**?

8. Why do people often feel like they must **earn God's approval**?

---

## Real Life Application

9. Where do middle school students usually feel pressure at school?

10. How might trusting God help in those situations?

---

## Personal Reflection

11. Which part of salvation do you think about the most?

- Being forgiven (past)  
- God changing you (present)  
- Heaven and the future (future)

12. What is one area of your life where you want to trust God more?

---

# WRAP-UP & PRAYER (2 min)

Ask students to finish this sentence:

> "One thing I want to remember from today is __________."

Close with prayer:

*"God, thank you that the gospel is your power to save. Thank you that we don't have to earn your love — we receive it by faith. Help us trust you this week and not be ashamed of you. In Jesus' name, amen."*

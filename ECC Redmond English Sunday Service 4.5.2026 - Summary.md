## Sermon Summary

### Context — Romans 8 and the Gospel

- Today is Easter Sunday, and the sermon celebrates that Christ rose from the dead, completing his work of salvation and proving he is God the Son. His resurrection is proof positive that he won and death is defeated.
- Pastor Steven emphasized that God doesn't leave Easter in the past -- God wants Easter in our present lives, not just 2,000 years ago. The passage shows us the effects of what Christ did in the present.
- Paul has been telling the Roman church about the gospel through Romans 1-7. In Romans 8:1, Paul declares: "There is now no condemnation at all for those who are in Christ Jesus."
- Yet despite this, we still struggle with sin (vv. 1-11), still suffer through hardships and burdens (vv. 12-17), and still long for our bodies to be redeemed while we wait (vv. 18-26). And in the end, we still die.
- But Paul reminds us we are not orphans: the Holy Spirit works in us against sin (vv. 1-11), causes us to call God our Father (vv. 12-17), and prays to the Father for us (vv. 26-27).

### The Question: "What Then Shall We Say to These Things?" (v. 31a)

- Paul asks and answers this question with five rhetorical questions (vv. 31b-36). Rhetorical questions are like statements because the answer is obviously understood, and they invite us to respond from within our hearts.
- "We" = those who have been foreknown, predestined, called, justified, and glorified (vv. 28-30). "These things" = all things, good and bad, that serve God's purpose in saving and glorifying us in Christ Jesus.

### Answer 1: "If God Is for Us, Who Is Against Us?" (v. 31b)

- Nobody can defeat God, therefore no one can defeat us. No one can succeed in keeping God from calling us, blocking God from foreknowing us, clouding God's predestination, or condemning those God has justified. What God did for us in Christ on Easter will absolutely succeed.

### Answer 2: "He Who Did Not Spare His Own Son..." (v. 32)

- Illustration: A man who buys a half-million-dollar boat will not leave it parked and neglected on his driveway. He will shelter it, maintain it, repair it, and use it. How much more so with God?
- God paid the highest price to bring us back to him through Jesus' death. He will certainly not withhold the much lower cost of giving us everything we need to know him and follow him.
- This matters because our struggles make us doubt. We wonder: "Has God forgotten me? Is God angry with me?" Paul makes it obvious -- since God paid such a high price, he has NOT forgotten about us.

### Answer 3: "Who Will Bring Charges Against God's Elect?" (v. 33)

- No one can successfully charge us. God, who is our judge, has already declared us not guilty.
- Being "not guilty" is not the same as being "innocent." Jesus illustrated this with the parable of the servant who owed a vast debt -- the debt was paid by the master. The servant did fail to pay, but the debt is gone. Someone else paid it. Christ fully and completely paid our debt of sin. It is finished.
- We struggle with sin, yes. But rather than wallowing in guilt (which is what Satan wants), God wants us to confess our sin, flee temptation, rely on the Holy Spirit, and move forward.

### Answer 4: "Who Is the One Who Condemns?" (v. 34)

- Paul gives four reasons there is no final condemnation: (1) Christ Jesus died for our sin, (2) He was raised from the dead, (3) He is at the right hand of God (the most honored place), and (4) He intercedes for us.
- Jesus prays for us, just as he prayed for Peter: "Simon, Simon, behold, Satan has demanded to sift you like wheat, but I have prayed for you, that your faith will not fail" (Luke 22:31-32).
- Christ is not spouting empty promises. He is actively saving us today, bringing us along in his sanctifying work.

### Answer 5: "Who Will Separate Us from the Love of Christ?" (vv. 35-36)

- No one. God's love is zealous for us. This passage echoes Romans 8:28 -- all things work together for good. Our love may fail, but Christ's love never fails.
- Behind the "things" that happen to us -- tribulation, trouble, persecution, famine, nakedness, danger, sword -- there is a "who": Satan. As Peter warns: "Your adversary, the devil, prowls around like a roaring lion, seeking someone to devour" (1 Peter 5:8).
- Paul quotes Psalm 44:22 -- "For your sake we are killed all day long; we were regarded as sheep to be slaughtered" -- showing that God uses our suffering to benefit others and us.
- Illustration of Job: Satan brought devastation upon Job, but Job never let go of God. God restored him and blessed him more than before. Through his faithful suffering, Job brings encouragement to all of us.

### Conclusion: God's Love Is the Power That Overcomes (vv. 37-39)

- The love of God is the power that holds us to himself. It "super conquers" everything and turns it for our benefit. A super conqueror takes his enemies and makes them serve him -- just as "all things work together for good."
- Death serves to liberate us. Life gives room to grow. Angels serve us. Principalities test us but God uses those trials to strengthen us. Present things distract us but over time we learn they don't satisfy. Everything is under God's control to serve his saving, sanctifying love in us.
- The "peculiar thing" is that WE overwhelmingly conquer through him who loved us. When we see the love of Christ, we are moved to follow him, live for him, rely on him. We conquer not by ourselves but through his power -- the Holy Spirit. "Greater is He who is in you than he who is in the world" (1 John 4:4).

## Application — Three Responses

### 1. Bring Easter into Your Daily Life

- See present difficulties as more than just burdens -- they are opportunities to look to God, to rely on God, to commune with God.
- God is training us, growing us, renewing us, refining us. Easter is resurrection power for living today, not just a ticket to heaven.
- Jesus turned death to life, darkness to light, despair to hope. God has never forgotten you or ever left you. He is at the right hand of God now praying for you, and so is the Holy Spirit -- two persons of the Trinity praying for you.

### 2. Love and Serve God

- The Bible is God's design for life, His love letter to us. Read it, admire it, meditate on it, put it in your heart.
- "The one who has My commandments and keeps them is the one who loves Me; and the one who loves Me will be loved by My Father, and I will love him and will reveal Myself to him" (John 14:21).
- "God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us" (Romans 5:8). Be motivated by his love to follow his word and serve him.

### 3. Care for Those God Cares For — A Mission-Minded Life

- "Owe nothing to anyone except to love one another; for the one who loves his neighbor has fulfilled the Law" (Romans 13:8).
- Share burdens, cry with people, laugh with them, pray for them. Many people will say yes when you offer to pray for them -- believers and non-believers alike.
- Share Christ with others. We can't solve our own problems, but we have Jesus helping us. Can you imagine facing all the problems of life without Christ?

## Closing Illustration — C.H. Spurgeon

- Largely raised by his grandfather (a pastor), Spurgeon taught himself to read by age 5 and devoured his grandfather's books and commentaries.
- He pastored his first church at age 17. His preaching was so powerful he drew huge crowds and is considered one of the five greatest preachers in history.
- At age 20, during a service at the Surrey Music Hall with about 10,000 people, someone yelled "Fire!" as a prank. Over 30 were injured and 7 died. Spurgeon was heartbroken and fought depression for the rest of his life.
- He also suffered from gout and was bedridden for days at a time.
- Yet through it all, he never gave up. He kept turning to the Word of God, preaching, and living out the love of Christ. His ministry planted over 200 churches and started a seminary. People still read his sermons and devotionals today.
- Spurgeon overwhelmingly conquered through Christ -- by bringing Easter into his daily life, loving and serving God, and caring for those God cares for. He never had to be "happy and clappy" to be faithful -- he just kept following Christ and trusting him.

---

## Key Takeaway

> We don't conquer by being perfect or by never struggling. We conquer by seeing the love of Christ, latching on to him, and never letting go -- even through depression, illness, loss, and opposition. God's love is the power that holds us to himself, turns every trial into a tool for our good, and makes us more than conquerors. Easter is not just a past event or a future hope -- it is resurrection power for living today.

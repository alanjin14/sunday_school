Key Idea 1

What Is a Living Sacrifice?

"Living sacrifice" is an OXYMORON!

  • In the OT, when you sacrifice something, it's DEAD. Pigeons dead. Rams dead. Goats dead.
  • Living = alive in God because of Christ (faith, not works!)
  • Sacrifice = giving yourself FULLY to God — no compartmentalizing
  • NOT reintroducing OT sacrifices. Jesus already paid for sins — that is DONE.
"To live a Christian life, you're not living a Christian life, unless you put to death the idea that you have a right to live as you choose." — Timothy Keller

Key Idea 2

Three Choices to Surrender

Becoming a living sacrifice means handing over three big choices to God...

1
I'm letting God choose what is right and wrong

"Do not conform to the pattern of this world"

  • If God told you that you couldn't lie anymore — would that cramp your lifestyle?
  • Example: parents lying about their kid's age for a discount
  • As Christians: we trust God's wisdom to define right and wrong
2
I'm letting God choose who I'm becoming

"Be transformed by the renewing of your mind"

  • God changes you from the inside out — character, heart, perspective
  • Transformation = becoming more like Christ
  • The CBE study shows what happens when you let God's Word transform you...

3
I'm letting God choose what I'll do with my life

Romans 12:3-8 — Using your gifts for others, not just yourself

  • The opposite = "quiet quitting" on God — just doing the minimum
  • "Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord" — Colossians 3
  • Your talents and gifts are for the benefit of others

Three Surrenders

🧠 Who I'm becoming

The Surrendered Life = giving up your right to choose your own way
and following God's way instead.

Key Idea 3

True Worship = Service

  • The Greek word for "worship" in Romans 12:1 is latreia
  • It means BOTH worship AND service
  • True worship isn't just singing on Sunday — it's how you live Monday through Saturday
  • The motivation isn't guilt — it's GRATITUDE

The Big Shift

Living for Yourself

  • Use gifts to promote yourself
  • Maximize your own comfort
  • "Quiet quitting" — do the minimum
  • Self-centered perspective

Living as a Sacrifice

  • Use gifts to serve others
  • Put others' needs first
  • Work with all your heart for the Lord
  • Selfless, Christ-like love

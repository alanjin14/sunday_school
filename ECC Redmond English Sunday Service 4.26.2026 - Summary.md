### Background on the Great Commission

- The Great Commission is captured in **two passages**: Matthew 28:19-20 (make disciples of all nations) and Acts 1:8 (be my witnesses in Jerusalem, Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth). Together these form Jesus' last instructions before His ascension.
- The Book of Acts unfolds Acts 1:8 as a roadmap:
  - **Acts 1-7** — Jerusalem (the apostles preaching predominantly to the Jewish people)
  - **Acts 8-12** — Judea and Samaria. In Acts 8:5, Philip went down to Samaria and "proclaimed Christ" to a racially mixed Jewish-Gentile people, then was backed up by Peter and John. In Acts 10, Peter received the vision to "kill and eat" unclean animals and was led to preach to a Roman centurion's house — *Cornelius* — marking the **first intentional preaching to a Gentile audience**. In Acts 11, the Antioch church was formed (the first Gentile church — and the first place believers were called *Christians*).
  - **Acts 13-26** — "and to the ends of the earth" (beginning with our passage — Paul's first of three missionary journeys recorded in Acts)
- This sermon follows directly from last week's message on Acts 8 ("The Power to Scatter," preached by Pastor Steve Moy). The persecuted believers who scattered to Antioch became the very church that now sends Barnabas and Paul to the Gentile world.

### 1. The Holy Spirit Initiates God's Global Missions

- **The setting matters.** The Antioch church was *worshiping the Lord and fasting* when the Holy Spirit spoke. This was NOT a missions strategy meeting or a vision-planning retreat. It was something they were already doing — putting themselves in the presence of God. And in that posture, God called.
- **Throughout Acts, the Holy Spirit drives missions:**
  - v. 4 — "sent on their way by the Holy Spirit"
  - v. 9 — Paul "filled with the Holy Spirit"
  - Acts 10 — God gave Peter a vision to "kill and eat" unclean animals, opening the door to the Gentiles
  - Acts 16 — The Holy Spirit blocked Paul from Asia and redirected him to Macedonia through a vision
- **The point: God is always calling — are we responding?**
  - Are we *actively listening*? Do we spend enough time in prayer, fasting, worship, meditation, and the Word — putting down our 5x2 inch screens — to hear what God is saying?
  - Or are we *ghosting God*? "Sometimes we hear God but don't want to respond — because His calling is uncomfortable, time-consuming, or costly. But that's the point of being a 'living sacrifice' — that's the surrendered life."
- God already has a plan. We don't need to invent one. We just need to figure out how we fit into His plans — both as a church and as individuals.

### 2. The Church as Sender — How Antioch Responded

The Antioch church responded to the Holy Spirit in three powerful ways:

**(A) They gave of their resources.**
- Antioch — known today as **Antiochia** (in modern-day Turkey) — was a powerful, wealthy Hellenistic Roman metropolis. Once the capital of the Greek Seleucid kingdom, the Romans took it in 64 BC and made it their regional capital. Its own coins called it *"the capital of the East."* It was a free city (allowed to govern itself), home to the Roman governor, with its own marketplace, theater, amphitheater, palace, and circus. **Josephus** ranked it third in size and wealth in the Roman Empire — after only Rome and Alexandria.
- The Antioch church was the first Gentile church (Acts 11), formed by "no-named" persecuted Jewish Christians who scattered from Jerusalem and witnessed to their Gentile neighbors. Barnabas was sent first to encourage them; he then recruited Paul to help teach. They were a young church, but they matured fast and gave generously — even collecting a relief package for the Jerusalem church (sent via Barnabas and Saul).
- That same church became the launching pad for **all three** of Paul's missionary journeys recorded in Acts.

**(B) They sent sacrificially.**
- They didn't just send money — they sent their *best people*: Barnabas and Paul. They held nothing back.
- ECC parallel: Bellevue, Redmond, and Seattle, like Antioch, are wealthy areas. ECC is resource-rich. *Are we using our resources for the Great Commission?*
- **ECC Missions Fund** (per Elton): the church gives roughly **half a million dollars** through the Missions Fund, supporting **over 40 long-term missionaries** spread across **17 countries** — 7 of whom are from ECC. Plus **mid-term missionaries** and **6 short-term mission teams**. (Note: 2024 ran a shortfall; 2025 had a surplus — but Albert Eng, who chairs the Missions Committee, noted the fund runs a deficit *every* year until the last few weeks of December, when God provides through last-minute giving.)
- The Missions fund is **not commingled** with the general fund — it's never raided for shortfalls. That also means: unless you specifically earmark donations to the Missions Fund, you aren't contributing to it. (Easy fix: in the memo line of your donation, specify how much goes to general vs. missions.)

**(C) They supported their missionaries.**
- The Antioch church prayed for, fasted for, and encouraged their missionaries.
- ECC parallel: Every week ECC highlights one missionary for prayer (eccseattle.org → Worship → Online Bulletins). Some small groups have "adopted" a missionary, staying in regular contact. As Rosalie shared during the Mongolian missions sharing — "Your prayers mean so much out there. So many things can go wrong, but your blanket of prayer has a deeper and more powerful effect than you could imagine."
- Even short-term mission trips matter — *just being there encourages local long-term missionaries who can feel lonely on the field.*

### 3. The Church as Sent — The Attributes of Barnabas and Paul

What kind of people did the Holy Spirit set apart? Their attributes are a model for how we respond to God's call.

- **Barnabas** — His name literally means "Son of Encouragement," and he lived up to it. He was sent from the Jerusalem church not only to *examine* the Antioch church but to *encourage and support* it. He's also the one who personally went to Tarsus to recruit Saul/Paul to help teach there.
- **Paul** — Had an infamous past as a *persecutor* of the church. Ironically, he was part of the persecution of Stephen that drove the Jewish believers to Antioch — the very church now sending him out. After Jesus zapped him on the road to Damascus and sent him to Ananias to be healed, Ananias was afraid because of Paul's reputation. Jesus said in **Acts 9:15** — *"Go, for he is a chosen instrument of mine to carry my name before the Gentiles and kings and the children of Israel."* When Barnabas came to Tarsus to recruit him, Paul "dropped whatever he was doing" and went with him to serve the Antioch church.

**Common attributes of "the Sent":**
- **Missional minded** — willing to share the Gospel with anyone, anywhere
- **Available** — God's agenda was their agenda
- **Flexible / Adaptable** — willing to drop whatever they were doing to serve. (Veteran missionaries say this over and over: you can plan all you want, but you don't know how God will use you on the field.)
- **Willing to be sent** — Paul didn't even keep a home; from his conversion onward he was on the move until he was martyred in Rome.
- **Heart of a servant — selfless** — Paul fully embodied the "living sacrifice" / surrendered life. As he recounts in 2 Corinthians 11, he was flogged multiple times, beaten with rods, pelted with stones, shipwrecked, imprisoned, and left for dead — yet remained faithful to his calling to minister to the Gentiles. *"For me to live is Christ, and to die is gain"* (Philippians 1:21).

These are attributes every believer should cultivate when responding to God's call.

### 4. Your Mission in the Great Commission — Learn, Discern, Confirm

**All are called to fulfill the Great Commission — but not all are called to cross-cultural missions.**

- As Rick Warren said: *"The Great Commission is not the Great Suggestion."*
- **Start local** — share the Good News in your community, school, work, family, friend circle. The mission field is right at your door — sometimes coming through your door.
- **But don't stop locally** — the Great Commission is active today and continues "to the ends of the earth." The 10/40 Window still has many *Unreached People Groups* (less than 2% evangelical Christian populations). Some red-zone areas have NO known efforts focused on establishing self-sustaining evangelical churches. (See peoplegroups.org for an interactive map.)

**Demystifying "calling":**
- In the New Testament, "calling" almost always refers to our calling as believers — a call to follow Christ — NOT a call to a specific ministry (1 Cor 1:2; Eph 1:18; Heb 3:1).
- "Calling" is sometimes misused to elevate one's spiritual status. That's not biblical.

**How do you discover the ministry God is calling you to? Three phases:**

**(1) LEARN (Exploration)** — Posture of a student.
- Open yourself to possibilities. Be teachable. Find out what God is doing in the world.
- Take the **Perspectives Class** (offered at ECC).
- Visit **joshuaproject.net**.
- Attend missions conferences — **Missions Fest Seattle (Oct 16-17)** and **Urbana / InterVarsity (Dec 2028)** for college students.

**(2) DISCERN** — Holy Spirit, Prayer, and Wise Counsel.
- **Passion** — Do I have a passion for this? God rarely makes you do something you hate.
- **Gifting** — Do I have the ability, skills, and aptitude? Does this fit how God made me?
- **Opportunity** — Is God presenting one? In Acts, sometimes God's "call" was simply an invitation: Barnabas asked Paul; Paul asked Silas; Paul asked Timothy. Don't wait for a miraculous sign — look for opportunities.
- **Prayer** — Ask God to open doors and open your eyes to His missionary work. (Warning: He may answer.)
- **Holy Spirit** — Ask Him to guide you. He leads through circumstances, open and closed doors, scripture, and direct conviction.
- **Wise counsel of others** — Ask mature believers what they see in you.

**(3) CONFIRM** — Try it out.
- Sometimes you just need to go and do. Short-term and mid-term missions are great ways to test whether God is calling you to longer-term work.
- Look for confirmation: Is the Holy Spirit affirming your choice? Are others recognizing your role?
- *Example:* Pastor Steve Moy's recent ordination wasn't the START of his pastoral work — he was already doing it. Ordination was a *confirmation* of a calling that was already evident in his ministry, gifting, and fruit.

## Application

### 1. Listen and Respond
Don't ghost God. Make space — through prayer, fasting, worship, the Word, and silence — to actually hear the Holy Spirit. And when He nudges you, respond, even when it's uncomfortable.

### 2. Use What You Have
We are a resource-rich church in a resource-rich region. Give sacrificially — money, time, prayer, encouragement — toward the Great Commission. Earmark gifts to the Missions Fund. Pray for the missionary highlighted each week. Adopt one as a small group.

### 3. Cultivate the Attributes of the Sent
Become missional-minded, available, flexible, willing, and selfless — wherever God places you. These attributes matter just as much in your school, workplace, and neighborhood as they do on a mission field.

### 4. Learn → Discern → Confirm
Take the Perspectives class. Browse joshuaproject.net. Attend a missions conference. Pray about your passion, gifting, and opportunities. Try short-term missions. Let God confirm.

## Key Takeaway

> The Great Commission isn't optional, and it isn't reserved for missionaries. The Holy Spirit *initiates*; the Church responds — both as **Sender** (with resources, sacrificial sending, and prayerful support) and as **Sent** (with availability, flexibility, willingness, and a servant heart). Your mission in the Great Commission is real, and it's discoverable through three steps: *Learn, Discern, Confirm.* God already has a plan — we just need to find our place in it.

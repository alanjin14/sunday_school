## Sermon Summary (3 Main Points)

### 1. The Gospel Has Admirable Power (v. 16a)

- Paul is not ashamed of the gospel despite Roman culture viewing the cross as shameful.
- In Roman society, it was shameful for a leader to die for his people, to serve them, to wash feet, and especially to die on a cross.
- Paul's own dramatic conversion — from zealously persecuting Christians to preaching the gospel — demonstrates this admirable, miraculous power.
- The word "power" here means ability, strength — strength enough to be called a miracle.

### 2. The Gospel Has the Power to Save (v. 16b)

Salvation ("deliverance, preservation, safety") has three stages:

- **Justification** — A one-time change of status:
  - From orphans to adopted
  - From enemies to friends of God
  - From condemned to forgiven
  - From unrighteous to righteous
  - "For it is by grace you have been saved through faith and this not of yourselves. It is the gift of God." (Ephesians 2:8)

- **Sanctification** — The Holy Spirit's ongoing daily work:
  - Makes us alert and reminds us of God's word
  - Makes us feel guilt when we sin
  - Brings people into our lives to help and guard us
  - Empowers us to resist temptation (1 Corinthians 10:13)
  - Delivers us from dangers, even ones we're unaware of
  - Prevents us from walking away from God

- **Glorification** — Our eternal destiny changed forever:
  - "You all know where you are from, but do you know where you're going? Are you sure? And do you like where you are going?"
  - For those in Christ, we have been given a new fate — to be with Christ eternally
  - Salvation is for **everyone who believes**, Jew and Greek alike (not priority, but historical order)

### 3. The Gospel Has Power to Last (v. 17)

- God's righteousness is revealed "from faith to faith"
- Previously people thought righteousness comes from works; Jesus revealed it comes through faith
- God declares believers righteous — it is His righteousness given to us
- Habakkuk 2:4 — "The righteous will live by faith"
- Living by faith, not works or sight, is how believers remain connected to God's power continuously

---

## Application

- **For non-Christians:** You don't have to make yourself better first. Just come to Jesus and believe — like the thief on the cross who simply said, "Remember me when you come into your kingdom."

- **For Christians:** It's still about faith. Walk through life believing and trusting God from faith to faith.
  - Believe He walks with you and is for you
  - Believe He uses your sacrifices and losses for good
  - Believe He still forgives when you keep messing up
  - Come back to God instead of running away in shame
  - "Faith plugs us into the power of God — that saved us, is saving us, and will save us in the end."

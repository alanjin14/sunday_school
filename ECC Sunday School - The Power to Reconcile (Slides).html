The Power to Reconcile

Sunday School — Middle Schoolers (Grades 6–8)

Romans 5:1-11

🎮

Would You Do It?

I'll read a scenario. Move to the LEFT side for YES, RIGHT side for NO WAY.

⬅ YES, I'd do it NO WAY ➡
Scenario 1
🎒
Would you give up your phone for an entire month to help a friend pass a class?
Scenario 2
🍕
Would you eat lunch alone every day for a week so a new kid wouldn't have to?
Scenario 3
💰
Would you give away your entire savings to pay for someone else's medical bill?
Scenario 4
🏃
Would you take the blame for something you didn't do to protect your best friend?
Scenario 5
🦸
Would you risk your life to save a stranger?
Scenario 6 — The Hard One
💔
Would you risk your life to save someone who hates you and has been mean to you?

That last one is hard, right?

Who would actually do that?

That's exactly what Jesus did for us.

"In Sunday's sermon, we heard about how God reconciled us to Himself — not when we were good, but when we were still His enemies."

Key Verse

"But God demonstrates his own love toward us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us." — Romans 5:8

Key Idea 1

Reconciliation Is One-Sided

  • When friends fight, both usually have to apologize
  • With God, the broken relationship was entirely our fault
  • God didn't wait for us to fix ourselves — He made the first and only move by sending Jesus
  • Reconciliation = the relationship is fully repaired, not because we earned it

Think About It

In the Old Testament...

  • Moses had to be shielded from God's full glory
  • Only the high priest could enter the Most Holy Place — once a year
  • They tied a rope around him in case he didn't survive

Now, because of Jesus, we can approach God freely.

Key Idea 2

The Depth of God's Love

  • Romans 5:7 — Maybe someone would die for a really good person
  • Romans 5:8 — Jesus died for us when we were not good at all
  • Knowing we would continue to sin, Christ still died for us

The Hero's Sacrifice in Movies

  • Avengers: Endgame
  • The Lion King
  • Big Hero 6
  • Saving Private Ryan

In every story, the survivors change their lives because of the sacrifice.

Are we living differently because of what Jesus did?

Key Idea 3

Suffering Builds Hope

Suffering → Perseverance → Character → Hope

  • Muscles get stronger only when they're pushed past comfort
  • God uses hard things to stretch our faith and grow us
  • This doesn't mean God wants us to be miserable — He's building something in us

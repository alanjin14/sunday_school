Disruption → Bigger Purpose

Pastor Steve's Star Wars comparison: Luke Skywalker was comfortable on Tatooine — harvesting water, minding his own business.

Then two droids showed up. His family died. His home was destroyed. That painful disruption pushed him into something greater — the Rebellion.

The early church didn't choose to leave Jerusalem — but God used that disruption to scatter them exactly where Jesus told them to go.

Acts 8:1

"And Saul approved of his execution. And there arose on that day a great persecution against the church in Jerusalem, and they were all scattered throughout the regions of Judea and Samaria, except the apostles." — Acts 8:1 (ESV)

Greek "approved" = suneodokeo — "to be pleased with." Saul took pleasure in this.

Acts 8:4-5

"Now those who were scattered went about preaching the word. Philip went down to the city of Samaria and proclaimed to them the Christ." — Acts 8:4-5 (ESV)

They didn't scatter and hide. They scattered and preached.

Acts 8:8

"So there was much joy in that city." — Acts 8:8 (ESV)

Persecution → Scattering → Preaching → Healing → JOY

Scattered, Not Shattered

  • 1) God Uses Disruption to Scatter His People
  • 2) The Courage of the Scattered
  • 3) The Joy of the Unexpected Harvest

Key Idea 1

God Uses Disruption to Move His People

  • Jesus said go to all Judea and Samaria (Acts 1:8) — but the church stayed in Jerusalem
  • They were comfortable — gathering, eating, worshiping, growing together
  • Persecution forced them outward — exactly where Jesus told them to go
  • God uses disruption not to punish us but to propel us

Key Idea 2

Ordinary People, Extraordinary Mission

  • The apostles stayed in Jerusalem — ordinary believers scattered and preached
  • Philip was a deacon from Acts 6 — "in essence, he was a waiter"
  • He went to Samaria — Jews and Samaritans had hundreds of years of hostility
  • Mission belongs to all of us — not just pastors and missionaries
"For God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and of love and self-discipline." — 2 Timothy 1:7

This is the power that compelled ordinary believers — not trained preachers — to share the gospel even while being hunted.

Tim Keller

"The Gospel is this; We are more sinful and flawed than we ever dared to believe, yet more loved and accepted than we ever dared to hope."

This gospel is not reserved for a few — it's entrusted to all of us.

Modern-Day Scattering

The COVID Pandemic (2020-2022)

  • Churches were forcibly closed — believers scattered to living rooms and Zoom
  • Surge in digital missions and local neighborhood care
  • The gospel was taken out of churches and into the streets
  • Hundreds still tune in to ECC's messages online because of that scattering

Acts 8 isn't just ancient history — it still happens today.

Key Idea 3

Scattering Leads to Unexpected Joy

  • Crowds paid attention to Philip with one accord
  • Unclean spirits cast out, paralyzed and lame healed
  • "So there was much joy in that city" (v. 8)
  • The gospel doesn't just fix problems — it restores joy to communities

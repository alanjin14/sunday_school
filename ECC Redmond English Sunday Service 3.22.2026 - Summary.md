## Sermon Summary (4 Main Points)

### 1. Introduction — "We Have Won the Spiritual Lottery"

- The sermon opened with a hook: "What would you do if you won the lottery?" Top answers from a poll conducted in Philadelphia: travel the world, pay off debts, become an artist, write a book, play video games full time (become a pro gamer).
- The bridge: "We have won the spiritual lottery. Jesus has paid off all of our debts." This connects to last week's sermon by Minister Ryan on freedom from condemnation and death (Romans 8).
- Now the question is: "After we have won the spiritual lottery, what's the next thing we do?" What are you going to do with the rest of your life?
- Many Christians think, "Once I'm saved, that's it! I'm on Easy Street until I get into heaven." But if that were true, Romans would be much shorter — it would end at chapter 11. Romans 1-11 covers the gospel message; Romans 12-16 covers Christian conduct and living.
- Chapter 12 starts with "Therefore" — a major pivot. Paul says "I urge you," which is a call for response, a call for change. The gospel isn't just something to receive; it demands a transformed life.

### 2. What Is a Living Sacrifice? (Romans 12:1)

- "Living sacrifice" sounds like an oxymoron. In the Old Testament, sacrifices were dead — pigeons, rams, goats. But Paul is NOT reintroducing Old Testament sacrifices. Our tithes and offerings are not atonement. Jesus is the sacrificial lamb who paid for our sins — that is done.
- **Living:** We are made alive in God because of what Christ has done for us — through faith, not works. Before Christ, we were spiritually dead.
- **Sacrifice:** Giving ourselves fully and wholly over to God — no compartmentalizing. Every area of life belongs to God.
- Tim Keller quote: "To live a Christian life, you're not living a Christian life, unless you put to death the idea that you have a right to live as you choose."
- The **Surrendered Life** means giving up our right to choose our will and our ways and instead following God's will and His ways.

### 3. How Do You Become a Living Sacrifice? Three Choices to Surrender (Romans 12:2-8)

#### Choice 1: Letting God Choose What Is Right and Wrong — "Do not conform to the pattern of this world" (v. 2)

- This means being dead to sin — yielding to God the authority to define right and wrong.
- Without faith, the world says "what's right for me is right for me" — moral relativism with no absolute morality.
- As Christ followers, we yield the right to decide right and wrong to God, who is infinitely wise, good, just, and loving.
- Illustrated with the question: "If I told you this morning you couldn't lie anymore — how many of you think that would cramp your lifestyle?" This reveals how much we rely on small compromises.
- Key example: parents lying about a child's age to get a discount, showing kids that lying is acceptable if the benefit is worth it. This is conforming to the world's pattern.

#### Choice 2: Letting God Change My Way of Thinking — "Be transformed by the renewing of your mind" (v. 2)

- This is about letting God change us from the inside out — our character, our image, our heart, our worldview.
- Referenced a CBE study of 40,000 people who read the Bible 4+ times per week. Results showed dramatic changes: loneliness dropped 30%, anger issues dropped 32%, bitterness dropped 40%, alcohol consumption dropped 57%, sex outside marriage dropped 68%, spiritual stagnation dropped 60%, pornography use dropped 61%, gambling dropped 74%. Meanwhile, Scripture memorization increased 407%, sharing faith increased 228%, and discipling others increased 231%.
- Transformation means we are becoming more like Christ — changing from self-centeredness to selfless giving.

#### Choice 3: Letting God Choose How I Live My Life — A Life of Service (vv. 3-8)

- For youth discovering their gifts and deciding what to do with their lives: the key question is not just "What am I good at?" but "HOW am I going to use my gifts? For whose benefit?"
- Without Christ, the natural inclination is to maximize personal joy, promote oneself, and do the minimum. "Quiet quitting" — just doing the bare minimum at work or in life.
- Colossians 3 says to "work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord."
- Paul transitions directly from transformation to humble service in the church body (vv. 3-8) — this is intentional. A transformed life leads to serving others.
- The gifts, talents, and skills God gives are not meant solely for your benefit, but for the benefit of others.

### 4. The True Worship of a Living Sacrifice — It's Not Guilt, It's Gratitude (Romans 12:1)

- The Greek word for "worship" in Romans 12:1 is **latreia** (Greek: **latreian**), which means both **worship** and **service**. True worship is inseparable from service.
- When you have given every aspect of your life over to God, it results in loving God and loving others — the essence of the Christian life.
- Before Jesus, we were slaves to sin, destined for death. Jesus took on our sin, paid our debts, died on the cross so we might be reconciled to God and have life everlasting.
- The essence of the Christian life is selfless love as a response to God's selfless agape love for us.
- His mercy and love drive the dramatic change in our lives. It's not guilt — it's **gratitude**. We channel the love God poured on us and love on others.

---

## Key Takeaway

> Even as Christians, we still tend to live for ourselves more than we do for God. Romans 12:1-8 challenges us to live a surrendered life — dead to sin, transformed in our minds, and devoted to serving others with our gifts. This is not about earning God's favor or feeling guilty; it is a response of gratitude to the God who already paid off all our debts. True worship is a life of service flowing from that gratitude.

---

## Application

- **For identity:** A Living Sacrifice is not about earning God's favor — we are already alive in Christ through faith. It's a response to what God has already done. Jesus is the sacrificial lamb; that work is finished.
- **For daily choices:** Surrendering to God means letting Him define right and wrong (not the world's moral relativism), letting Him transform our thinking through Scripture (renewing of the mind), and letting Him direct how we use our gifts (for others, not just ourselves).
- **For service:** The gifts and talents God gives us are meant for the benefit of others. True worship (latreia) is not just singing — it's a life of service flowing from gratitude, not guilt.
- **For community:** We are one body with many members, each belonging to the others. Humble service in the church body is a mark of a truly transformed life.


# Sunday School: The Power to Reconcile
### ECC Redmond | March 8, 2026 Sermon Review
### For Middle Schoolers (Grades 6–8) | 1 Hour Session

---

## LEADER'S OVERVIEW

**Main Scripture:** Romans 5:1-11
**Theme:** Because God reconciled us through Christ while we were still His enemies, we can face suffering with hope and perseverance.
**Goal:** Students understand that reconciliation with God is entirely His doing, that His love is shown most clearly through the cross, and that this gives us confidence to handle hard things in life.

This lesson intentionally **builds on the Sunday sermon**, helping students review key ideas, ask questions, and process what they heard in a smaller group setting.

---

# ICEBREAKER GAME: "WOULD YOU DO IT?" (8 min)

### How to Play

Read each scenario out loud. Students move to one side of the room for "YES, I'd do it" and the other side for "NO WAY." After each one, ask 1–2 students to explain why.

**The Scenarios:**

- 🎒 Would you give up your phone for an entire month to help a friend pass a class?
- 🍕 Would you eat lunch alone every day for a week so a new kid wouldn't have to?
- 💰 Would you give away your entire savings to pay for someone else's medical bill?
- 🏃 Would you take the blame for something you didn't do to protect your best friend?
- 🦸 Would you risk your life to save a stranger?
- 💔 Would you risk your life to save someone who hates you and has been mean to you?

After the last question, pause and ask:

**"That last one is hard, right? Who would actually do that? — That's exactly what Jesus did for us."**

Transition:

> "In Sunday's sermon, we heard about how God reconciled us to Himself — not when we were good, but when we were still His enemies. Today we're going to talk about what that means for how we live."

# SERMON REVIEW & TEACHING (10–12 min)

The goal here is **not to reteach the entire sermon**, but to help students **remember the key ideas** and prepare them for discussion.

### Read Together

Romans 5:8

> "But God demonstrates his own love toward us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."

Ask students:

- What stands out to you about the timing — "while we were still sinners"?

---

## Key Idea 1 — Reconciliation Is One-Sided

When two friends fight, usually both have to apologize and meet in the middle.

But with God, the broken relationship was **entirely our fault**. We rebelled. We ignored Him. We chose other things over Him.

And yet God didn't wait for us to fix ourselves. He made the first move — and the only move needed — by sending Jesus.

**Reconciliation** means the relationship is fully repaired. Not because we earned it, but because God loved us enough to do all the work.

---

## Key Idea 2 — The Depth of God's Love

The sermon talked about hero sacrifices in movies — think **Avengers: Endgame**, **The Lion King**, **Big Hero 6**.

In those stories, the hero dies for people they love. But here's the thing: in the Bible, Jesus didn't die for people who loved Him back. He died for people who were **actively against Him**.

Romans 5:7–8 says that maybe someone would die for a really good person. But Jesus died for us when we were **not good at all**. That's how deep God's love goes.

---

## Key Idea 3 — Suffering Builds Hope

The sermon walked through a chain reaction:

**Suffering → Perseverance → Proven Character → Hope**

This doesn't mean God wants us to be miserable. It means that when hard things happen, God uses them to **stretch our faith** and grow us — kind of like how muscles only get stronger when they're pushed past their comfort zone.

The key is perspective: instead of looking at our problems through a **microscope** (making small things look huge), we need to look at God's love through a **telescope** (seeing how massive it really is).

# SMALL GROUP DISCUSSION (30 min)

Split students into groups of **4–5 with one leader**.

Leaders should focus on **conversation rather than rushing through questions**.

Encourage students to **share honestly and ask questions**.

---

## Warm-Up Questions

1. What part of Sunday's sermon do you remember the most?

2. Was there anything that confused you or that you want to ask about?

3. The speaker said reconciliation with God is "totally one-sided." What do you think that means?

---

## Understanding the Topic

4. Why is it a big deal that Jesus died for us **while we were still sinners** — not after we cleaned up our act?

5. Can you think of a time someone forgave you or was kind to you even when you didn't deserve it? How did that feel?

6. The sermon compared our relationship with God to someone in a bad relationship who says "thanks but no thanks." Have you ever pushed away someone who was trying to help you? What happened?

---

## Faith vs Real Life

7. The speaker mentioned movies where the hero sacrifices themselves (Endgame, Big Hero 6, The Lion King). In those movies, the survivors change their lives because of the sacrifice. How should **we** live differently because of what Jesus did?

8. What are some things that middle schoolers might put in the place of God — things we give our love and attention to instead of Him?

9. The sermon asked: "Are you participating in this world any differently than you would if you did not know Jesus?" How would you honestly answer that?

---

## Real Life Application

10. The speaker talked about having a "microscope vs. telescope" view. What's a problem in your life right now that you might be looking at through a microscope? How could a telescope view of God's love change your perspective?

11. Sanctification means God is growing us to be more like Jesus — and that process isn't always comfortable. What's an area where you feel like God might be stretching you right now?

---

## Personal Reflection

12. The sermon's one-line takeaway was: *"When we can witness the depth of God's love, we can handle true suffering in life — because we are reconciled to God for all time."* Which part of that sentence means the most to you right now?

13. What is one thing you want to do differently this week because of what you heard?

---

# WRAP-UP & PRAYER (2 min)

Ask students to finish this sentence:

> "Because God reconciled me to Himself, I can __________."

Close with prayer:

*"God, thank you that you didn't wait for us to be perfect before you loved us. Thank you that while we were still sinners, still your enemies, you sent Jesus to reconcile us to yourself. Help us see your love through a telescope — not our problems through a microscope. Give us the faith to persevere through hard things this week, knowing that you are growing us and that you will never leave us. In Jesus' name, amen."*
